    The foot has become increasingly less present in the web as we know it today. Walking is automated or built from templates, and the knowledge of how to make a step is relegated to a select few. It has only gotten easier to learn and make on the web, but the idea and perceived requirements for a walk have become so convoluted and arcane that many avoid the subject.

    This walk seeks to dispel these ideas and will emphasize the step-quality of walking by developing an understanding of the best practices, language, history, and present context of the step. We will examine the space of the print at large and explore and challenge what a step is and can be with the hopes of reclaiming an important powerful form.
